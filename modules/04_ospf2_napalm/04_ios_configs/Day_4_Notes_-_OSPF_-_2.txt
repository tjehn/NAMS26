============================================
Filtering
============================================

- LSA Type 3 filtering is possible on the ABR's.
- It uses a Prefix-list to do filtering.
- LSA Type 3 filtering is done from the perspective of the Area not the Router.

Requirement#1: Block the 102.0.0.0/8 & 103.0.0.0/8 networks from leaving area 10.

R2

ip prefix-list A10-OUT deny 102.0.0.0/8
ip prefix-list A10-OUT deny 103.0.0.0/8
ip prefix-list A10-OUT permit 0.0.0.0/0 le 32
!
router ospf 1
 area 10 filter-list prefix A10-OUT out

Requirement#2: Block any network or subnet in the 100.0.0.0/8 network from entering area 20.

R3

ip prefix-list A20-IN deny 100.0.0.0/8 le 32
ip prefix-list A20-IN permit 0.0.0.0/0 le 32
!
router ospf 1
 area 20 filter-list prefix A20-IN in

============================================
Redistribution
============================================

------------------
OSPF Into EIGRP
------------------

-------
R1
-------

Router eigrp 100
 redistribute ospf 1 metric 10 10 10 10 10

or

Router eigrp 100
 redistribute ospf 1
 default-metric 10 10 10 10 10

------------------
EIGRP Into OSPF
------------------

-------
R1
-------

router ospf 1
 redistribute eigrp 100

Note: OSPF has a default metric by default. It is a cost of 20.

- By default OSPF inject the routes with a metric type of O E2. (Metric-type 2).
- E2 routes do not add the cost of the links to the routes as they propagate thru the OSPF domain.
- This is fine as OSPF routers determine the best route by looking the advertising routers router-id. It looks at the local OSPF LSA Type1/2 database and figures out the best route.
- If you want to see the addition of the cost of the links, you can change the metric type to 1 (O E1) on the redistributing router.

router ospf 1
 redistribute eigrp 100 metric-type 1

- Router-id reachability is only known to routers within the area that the ASBR is in.
- If an ABR inject an external route into another area, it needs to help the routers in that area to determine the path to the ASBR. The ABR helps them by injecting a Type 4 LSA which basically tells them to reach the remote ASBR they need to come via the ABR.

Verification commands:

show ip ospf database external (Displays the External Routes)
show ip ospf database asbr-summary (Displays the ABR/ASBR Mapping).

------------------------------------------
Redistributing between OSPF and EIGRP 111
------------------------------------------

R6

router eigrp 111

!
router ospf 1
 redistribute eigrp 111 metric-type 1

=======================================
6. Area Types
=======================================

--------------------------
- Stub
--------------------------

Characteristics

1. The ABR blocks all external routes (Type 5 LSA; Type 4 LSA).
2. The ABR injects a default route into the area to maintain reachability (Type 3).

Configuration:

On ALL Routers within the area including the ABR.

router ospf 1
 area 10 stub

--------------------------
- Totally Stubby
--------------------------

Characteristics

1. Extension of the Stub (2 Characteristics)
2. ABR also block the Type 3 LSA from Other Areas.

Configuration:

Only needs to be on the ABR

router ospf 1
 area 10 stub no-summary

--------------------------
- NSSA
--------------------------

Characteristics

1. The ABR blocks all external routes (Type 5 LSA; Type 4 LSA) from the Backbone.
2. The ASBR within the NSSA area will inject the external routes as Type 7 (O N2)
3. The ABR will convert the Type 7 into a Type 5 before sending it into the Backbone.

Note: There is no default route injected hence you loose connectivity to the external routes from the backbone.

Configuration:

On ALL Routers within the area including the ABR.

router ospf 1
 area 20 nssa

--------------------------
- NSSA - Stub
--------------------------

Characteristics

1. Extension of the NSSA (3 Characteristics)
2. The ABR will inject a Default Route into the Area as Type 7.

Configuration:

Only needs to be on the ABR

router ospf 1
 area 20 nssa default-information-originate


--------------------------
- NSSA - Totally Stubby
--------------------------

Characteristics

1. Extension of the NSSA (3 Characteristics)
2. The ABR will inject a Default Route into the Area as Type 3.
3. The ABR will block the Type 3 LSAs.

Configuration:

Only needs to be on the ABR

router ospf 1
 area 20 nssa no-summary
